# Xiangrui Whiteboard Recorder Guide

Xiangrui Whiteboard Recorder is built for recording whiteboard tutorials, course demos, and async walkthrough videos. The usual flow is simple: prepare the board, choose the recording frame, then start recording and export the video.

## Quick Start

1. Draw on the whiteboard, or import components from the library.
2. Click **Record** in the top-right controls to choose the recording frame.
3. Pick `16:9`, `4:3`, `3:4`, `9:16`, `1:1`, or a custom size.
4. Drag the purple frame to reposition it, or drag corners to resize it.
5. Click **Start recording**, then export the video when finished.

## Recording Settings

Recording settings control the final video canvas:

- **Aspect ratio**: Fit YouTube, courses, vertical videos, square content, or custom frames.
- **Background**: Choose a built-in background or upload your own image.
- **Corner radius**: Adjust the recording canvas corner style.
- **Canvas padding**: Control the spacing between content and video edges.
- **Camera**: Choose a video source, switch circle or square, and adjust size.
- **Audio**: Choose microphone source and control microphone or sound capture.
- **Cursor**: Control cursor highlight during recording.

## Camera

The camera bubble appears near the lower-right board area by default. Click it to open quick controls:

- Switch between circle and square.
- Adjust camera size.
- Select the camera source from recording settings.

To use an iPhone Continuity Camera, first make sure macOS exposes the iPhone as a camera device, then select it from the camera source list.

## Library

Click the library button in the top-right controls to open the side library panel. The library supports:

- Searching public libraries.
- Refreshing the official library list.
- Adding assets to the current board.
- Downloading library files.

## Shortcuts

Shortcut settings are available from the top-left menu. You can customize actions such as:

- Start or confirm recording.
- Cancel the recording frame.
- Pause or resume recording.
- Open recording settings.
- Open the library.
- Toggle the teleprompter.
- Toggle grid, zen mode, view mode, and other editor preferences.

## Open Source Thanks

Whiteboard editing is powered by [Excalidraw](https://github.com/excalidraw/excalidraw), while recording and media handling use multiple open-source projects. Third-party sources, licenses, and modification notes are maintained in the project documents.
